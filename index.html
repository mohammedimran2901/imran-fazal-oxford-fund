His Journey & Impact

A Scholar for Our Times: Bridging Worlds

Imran Fazal represents a unique blend of profound traditional Islamic scholarship, rigorous philosophical training, and critical experience in public service. His journey embodies a commitment to deep learning and practical application for societal good.

Profile & Contributions

Imran Fazal is a distinguished academic and public servant. Holding a BA and B.Phil in Philosophy from the University of Oxford, he further deepened his knowledge through a decade of intensive traditional Islamic studies in Damascus, Syria, where he earned teaching licenses (ijāzāt) in Islamic law, theology, and hadith. This was followed by a Higher Licence in Islamic Law from al-Azhar University, Cairo.

Beyond academia, Imran has served at the highest levels of the UK civil service, including as Deputy Head of the Joint Extremism Unit and Head of Assessments for the Extremism Analysis Unit within the Home Office and Ministry of Justice. In these roles, his expertise in classical Arabic and Islamic thought was critical in informing national counter-terrorism strategies, directly impacting societal safety and understanding.

The DPhil Project

Re-evaluating Ibn Taymiyya's Doctrine of Jihad

Imran's doctoral research focuses on the doctrine of jihād in the writings of the medieval Muslim jurist Ibn Taymiyya (d. 1328). This project is not merely an academic exercise; it delves into a historical figure whose complex legacy is often misconstrued in contemporary discourse, particularly concerning radical ideologies.

Core Thesis

Imran aims to demonstrate that Ibn Taymiyya, later in his life, argued for a doctrine of jihād primarily rooted in self-defence or the inability to freely practice Islam. This represents a significant departure from prevalent medieval views that often permitted proactive warfare.

Timely Relevance

By providing a rigorous, text-based re-evaluation, the research directly challenges contemporary extremist interpretations that selectively appropriate Ibn Taymiyya's authority, offering a vital academic counter-narrative.

Supervisors & Academic Rigour

Imran will be jointly supervised by two leading authorities in the field:

  • Professor Nicolai Sinai FBA: Professor of Islamic Studies, University of Oxford, Fellow of Pembroke College. His expertise in Qur’anic studies and Islamic philosophical thought is invaluable.
  • Professor Fitzroy Morrissey: Fellow of All Souls College, Oxford. His research interests in Islamic law and history, particularly reformist thought, align perfectly with Imran’s project.

Beyond Academia

Impact: Community & Independent Islamic Thought

This DPhil is more than just a thesis; it is an investment in fostering a more nuanced understanding of Islamic tradition, directly addressing contemporary challenges, and cultivating independent, critical Islamic research for the benefit of global Muslim communities and broader society.

Strengthening Public Discourse & Counter-Extremism

Imran's background in both traditional Islamic studies and counter-terrorism makes his research uniquely positioned to inform and enrich public discourse. By meticulously reconstructing Ibn Taymiyya's authentic views on jihād, the project directly contributes to efforts to:

  • Undermine extremist narratives: Provide scholarly correctives against selective and distorted readings of classical texts used to justify violence.
  • Promote peace: Highlight historical precedents within Islamic thought for defensive rather than offensive warfare, fostering interpretations that align with peaceful coexistence.
  • Inform policy: Offer evidence-based insights derived from deep textual engagement, valuable for policymakers working on national security and interfaith relations.

This work will generate much-needed perspectives at the intersection of religious scholarship and policy, helping to build bridges rather than walls of misunderstanding.

Cultivating Independent Islamic Research

The academic landscape for Islamic studies in the Western world, especially for scholars deeply rooted in traditional learning, is still evolving. Supporting Imran Fazal's DPhil signifies a commitment to:

  • Intellectual independence: Championing scholars who can critically engage with both classical Islamic texts and Western academic methodologies, fostering objectivity and intellectual integrity.
  • Nuanced scholarship: Moving beyond simplistic or polemical interpretations by establishing rigorous, text-based research that speaks with authority to both religious and academic audiences.
  • Future leadership: Nurturing future generations of Islamic scholars equipped to address complex contemporary issues from within a well-grounded, multi-disciplinary framework.

Such independent scholarship is vital for the development of resilient, self-critical, and vibrant Islamic thought that can contribute positively to global intellectual and social life.
